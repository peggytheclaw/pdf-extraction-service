## Email Best Practices

### Subject Line Guidelines
‚úÖ **DO:**
- Keep it under 50 characters
- Make it personal (use their name or company)
- Reference where you found them
- Be specific (not vague)
- Create curiosity without being clickbaity

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Use all caps
- Use excessive punctuation (!!!)
- Make promises you can't keep
- Use generic templates ("Business Opportunity!")
- Be misleading

### Body Copy Guidelines
‚úÖ **DO:**
- Keep it under 150 words (busy people skim)
- Use whitespace and bullets
- Ask ONE clear question
- Include ONE clear call-to-action
- Personalize every email (mention something specific)
- Proof read (typos kill credibility)

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Write essays (nobody reads them)
- Use industry jargon
- Talk about yourself too much
- Multiple CTAs (confusing)
- Attach files in first email (spam filters)

### Timing Best Practices
- **Best days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times**: 8-10 AM or 1-3 PM (recipient's timezone)
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings (inbox overload), Friday afternoons (weekend mode)
- **Follow-up cadence**: Day 0, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30

### Personalization Checklist
Before sending ANY email:
- [ ] First name spelled correctly
- [ ] Company name mentioned
- [ ] Specific reference to where you found them
- [ ] Reference to their specific pain point
- [ ] Relevant detail from their profile/post
- [ ] Call-to-action matches their situation
